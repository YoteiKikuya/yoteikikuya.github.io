Beatrix, Queen of Remnants
Character Design Project

This project focuses on redesigning Beatrix, Queen of Remnants, a character I initially created in 2019. The final outcome is a cohesive design sheet that reflects her evolved narrative role as a capable leader and protector, blending regality and ruggedness.

The redesign was a personal project, aimed at enhancing my skills in character design and iterative workflows. The project spanned a week in the summer of 2024, with Krita as my application of choice. My roles included sketching, iteration, and refinement of Beatrixâ€™s design, focusing on silhouette exploration, prop design, and narrative alignment.

The objective was to redesign Beatrix to align her appearance with her narrative role. Her original design no longer reflected her story, creating a disconnect between her visual identity and character traits. This redesign aimed to bridge that gap, emphasizing her qualities as a leader and protector.

The process began by identifying non-negotiable design elements: a prosthetic arm, a dark complexion, and dual-wielded weapons. I explored 21 iterations in total, experimenting with silhouettes, climates, and aesthetics to push creative boundaries. After selecting iteration 5B as the foundation, I refined the design by integrating features from other iterations , such as hairstyle 2C and prosthetic arm components from versions F and H. Props were carefully designed to enhance cohesion and add visual intrigue. The final design achieves a balance of practicality and narrative alignment, while maintaining aesthetic appeal.

As the sole creative lead on this project, I gained a deeper appreciation for the iterative design process and the importance of self-evaluation. Working independently allowed me to refine my ability to assess design choices critically and adapt them to achieve a cohesive result. However, I recognize the value of incorporating feedback and working within client requirements to align creative outcomes with specific needs. Moving forward, I aim to engage in projects that emphasize collaboration, feedback loops, and client-driven goals to expand my versatility as a designer and improve my ability to meet diverse expectations.
