AUTOVISCERA
Animated Short


An ancient automaton’s scanners boot up for the first time in uncounted centuries. It projects the discovery of something remarkable: Purely biological life, able to exist independently of machinery. Witnessing this discovery is a Courier, a meek machine designed to transport lifeforce across battlefields and be consumed by the recipient. For the first time since the Great Feasting, it forms a goal independent of its function. In order to witness this life, it must trek across the wasteland between the citadel and outer wall, where machines wage perpetual war, long having outlived their original purpose. Upon reaching the Citadel, the danger doesn’t end. The Courier must navigate infrastructure never designed to be navigated, addicts who capture Couriers to siphon their energy to feel more alive than the living, and mad guards that crave violence to retain semblance of duty.

In the end, the Courier reaches its destination, beaten down and exhausted by its journey. It collapses, content to fade in lush moss in place of scorched bedrock. As its vessel fails, its remaining lifeforce leaks out into the soil, planting the seed for a brighter future.
