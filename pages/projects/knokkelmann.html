Knokkelmann
IAT 167 Game Design Project


Purpose: School Project
Category: Game Design
Software: Processing, Krita
Date: 17/11/2024 - 08/12/2024

For my final project for my IAT 167: Digital Games course, I developed Knokkelmann (Bone Man), a top-down shooter game inspired by metal music and Nordic folklore. This project has two versions, the first being a prototype that aimed to showcase the functionality of the basic mechanics, but lacking much distinction or visual assets. The second, final version includes more enemy variety, the powerup mechanic, a more compelling projectile system, menus and an aesthetic theme.

As per the course’s requirements, I programmed Knokkelmann in Java, using the Processing program and its built-in functionality. To create the sprites, I hand-drew them on Krita at the resolution I intended for the final product, to avoid problems with scaling. I had three weeks to go from conception to launch. I spent the first week developing the prototype, then the following two solidifying a theme and developing on the foundation I had built. There were basic game and code requirements for full marks, such as three distinct enemies, a powerup system and the main menu and game over screens. Additionally, there were bonus marks awarded to extra mechanics and functionality. To accomplish this, I modified the boss enemy (Bjergtrolde)’s behaviour for distinct movement patterns and modified the player’s thrown projectiles into a more unique boomerang that could be used more strageically than the standard bullet. Additionally, I implemented a difficulty scaling of sorts, where more challenging enemies would begin appearing after the player reached a certain number of points.

Seeing as I worked on this project solo, I had to budget my time to create a product that fulfilled the requirements, not necessarily the result I would have preferred. This mainly reflects the visual side, as I did not have time to make animations for character actions. Implementing this would have not only have served for aesthetic appeal, but also for more intuitive gameplay in regard to making it clear when the player or an enemy was hit. Despite this, my instructor was satisfied by the result, and I completed the project with a 94/100. With this in mind, I will prioritize the demands of the client before my own feelings in future projects, and make sure those are all met in a timely matter before exploring and discussing areas for further improvement.
