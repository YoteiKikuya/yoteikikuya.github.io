My final project in IAT 380 (Character Design) was to create a comprehensive overview of an original character, exploring ideation, detail exploration and working within the constraints of our chosen medium. I deicded to design a boss enemy in a 3D action game.

My character, Iersza, is the emperor of a metallurgical civilization, whose body had been willingly transformed into molten gold, to give his people a god to rally behind. With this transformation, he was granted control over metal, which he uses to hold a humanoid form. In order to guide my design process, I formulated a design inquiry:

What makes a character look godly?

Hands-Free Power

In a genre as saturated as fantasy action games, it was important that I choose an unconventional, yet engaging approach to combat. Despite his physical stature, Iersza’s power set is ultimately that of a mage. His attacks are seldom feats of physical strength, but psychic mastery, seen in how he commandeers his weapon. Thumbnails featured a conduit weapon, but I opted out in favor of the power being all his own.

Balance and Momentum

Early on, I struggled with designing a physically imposing frame, which ended up looking hunched, brutish or bloated. Further, they lacked the mobility that someone like Iersza should have. Straightening his shoulders, extending his legs and adding a waistcloth to widen his base struck a balance of appearing indomitable and in control of his own movement.

A Living Crown

I communicated Iersza’s authority being inseparable from his being by physically merging the two. His head and shoulders make up the crown imagery, showing that he has let his role consume him.

Mythology Made Physical

I took several real-world mythological motifs that fit Iersza’s character to reinforce the link between this contemporary mythology and traditional views of the design. His wreath is inspired by Raijin, the Japanese god of thunder, combined with a model of geocentrist belief. Iersza’s power represents the earth, and the star-like ornaments that orbit his head constantly are declarations of universal authority and control.
