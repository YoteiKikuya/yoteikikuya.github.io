AUTOVISCERA

Project Type:
Animated Short Film
Organization:
Simon Fraser University
Roles:
Creative Direction, Environment Modelling, Character Modelling, Animation, Concept Art, Storyboarding, Writing, Texturing, Graphic Design
Software:
Maya, Unreal Engine, Krita
Duration:
September 2025 - December 2025
Collaborators:
Pouya Salehi, Warren Viljoen, Cian Ho

My final project in IAT 339 Animation was to produce a 3D animated short from treatment to final cut, with the majority of work being done in Maya and Unreal Engine. I decided the premise with a logline and several pieces of concept art to motivate direction:

A tiny robot must navigate a hostile machine empire to deliver essential energy to the first biological life in millennia.

Due to the project’s scale, asset creation began immediately. Since our narrative was made up of a series of notable encounters, we each decided our own parts based on personal interest, each of us taking one major and one minor section. This compartmentalization allowed us to focus on our own parts without stepping over each other’s toes creatively. Environmental assets were shared between every member. I provided reference material and artwork for each character, then began developing my main sequences, the bird chase and laser cannon.

The main inspiration for this sequence was Alfred Hitchcock’s South by Southwest, carrying over the mechanical properties of a fighter plane combined with the uncanny, animalistic behaviors of a clockwork bird. A key inquiry here was the distinction between biology and machinery, and how it would work when those lines were blurred. I built the bird from several parts to allow for unnatural movements such as appendages moving along rails or vertebrae spinning on axes.

In addition to my sequence, I regularly met with other group members to monitor progress and alignment with creative direction. I oversaw lighting, shot composition, pacing and clear narrative communication.

Retrospective

With the structuring at the time of the final edit, the seams between who worked on which scenes are quite apparent. If I were to continue working on this film, I would shift work allocation to specialized, overarching roles. In my case, that would be stepping away from active animation to instead offering more comprehensive oversight and reference material, to ensure that nobody is left in the dark at any point in production, which with the timescales we were working at, every hour was essential.
